George Floyd Bio, Age, Death, Adult Life, Education &amp; More

Who is George Floyd?

George Perry Floyd Jr. is an African-American native who was murdered by a police officer in Minneapolis, Minnesota during an arrest.

Floyd was born on October 14, 1973 in Fayetteville, North Carolina in the United States of America.

Floyd’s mother relocated with the children to the Cuney Homes public housing, often known as the Bricks, in Houston’s Third Ward, a predominantly African-American neighborhood, when Floyd was two years old after his parents divorced.

Being over six feet (183 cm) tall in middle school, Floyd, who was known as Big Floyd as a child, regarded sports as a means of enhancing his life.

Floyd attended Ryan Middle School and received his high school diploma from Yates in 1993. He was a power forward on the basketball team at Yates and served as co-captain. As a tight end for the football squad, he participated in the 1992 Texas State finals with his team.

Floyd was the first of his siblings to attend college, spending two years at South Florida Community College on a football scholarship while also participating in basketball.

In 1995, he changed schools and enrolled at Texas A&M University-Kingsville, where he played basketball as well.

After being introduced to one another in the mid-1990s, Floyd became friends with Stephen Jackson, a future NBA star who was known as his “twin” due to their striking likeness.

He was 6 feet 6 inches (198 cm) at his tallest and 6 feet 4 inches (193 cm) at the time of his autopsy. He weighed 223 pounds (101 kg) at that time.

George Floyd death

Derek Chauvin, a white Minneapolis police officer, killed Floyd by keeping Floyd’s knee to his neck for 9 minutes and 29 seconds while Floyd was lying face down in the roadway in handcuffs.

As Floyd pleaded repeatedly that he could not breathe, as seen in a witness’ cellphone video, three other officers forcibly restrained him, and a fourth officer blocked bystanders from interfering.

Floyd was immobile and without a pulse for the final two minutes, but Chauvin continued to support Floyd’s neck and back even as emergency medical personnel came to administer aid.

George Floyd adult life

In 1995, Floyd moved back to Houston after finishing college in Kingsville, Texas, where he worked as an auto customizer and participated in club basketball.

He began performing as a rapper in the hip-hop group Screwed Up Click in 1994 under the alias Big Floyd. Floyd received eight jail stints between 1997 and 2005 for a variety of offenses, including drug use, theft, and trespassing.

Based solely on the testimony of police officer Gerald Goines, Floyd was found guilty in one of these cases in 2004 of carrying less than half a gram of crack cocaine.

Due to Goines’ involvement in the Harding Street raid and subsequent investigation for a pattern of fabricating evidence, the Harris County, Texas, district attorney requested a posthumous pardon for Floyd’s conviction in April 2021 due to Goines’ lack of credibility.

In 2007, Floyd was accused of aggravated robbery with a deadly weapon. According to investigators, Floyd and five other men broke into the flat by pretending to be water department workers. He then put a pistol to a woman’s stomach while looking for valuables to steal.

Three months later, during a traffic check, Floyd was taken into custody, and robbery victims recognized him from a photo array. As part of a plea agreement, Floyd received a five-year prison term in 2009 and was released on parole in January 2013.

Following his release, Floyd grew closer to the Christian church and ministry Resurrection Houston, where he mentored young men and shared anti-violence messages on social media.

2014 saw Floyd go to Minneapolis in an effort to start over and find employment. He quickly finished a 90-day rehabilitation program at the Turning Point program in north Minneapolis after arriving.

Floyd admitted that he needed a job and started working as a security guard at Harbor Light Center, a Salvation Army shelter for the homeless.

After receiving a ticket for operating a vehicle without a current commercial license and being involved in a minor collision, he was fired from his delivery driver employment in January.

When the COVID-19 pandemic struck Minnesota, he was looking for a new job, and when the club closed in March owing to pandemic regulations, his financial condition got worse.
