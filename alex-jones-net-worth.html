He has written books and sold products on his website worth hundreds of millions of dollars. His products include everything from “brain pills” to survival gear.

Alex Jones: Early Life Details

Jones was born in Dallas, Texas, on February 11, 1974. He grew up in the nearby town of Rockwell. He was raised in Austin after that. His dad is a dentist, and his mom stays at home and takes care of him. In 1993, he finished high school at Austin’s Anderson High School. He went to Austin Community College after that, but he didn’t finish.

Alex Jones: Personal Life Details

Jones and his ex-wife Kelly Jones broke up in 2015. They had three kids together. In 2017, her ex-husband Kelly asked for sole custody or joint custody of their children. She said he wasn’t a stable person and was involved in crime. Kelly has been given the power to decide where their children will live. He will still be able to see them, but it will be up to Kelly.

Jones held a rally at the Capitol in 2020, where he was surrounded by supporters and called COVID-19 a hoax. After this, a state district judge denied Kelly’s urgent request to get custody of their daughters for the next two weeks. His son, Rex Jones, has worked for InfoWars before. Jones married Erika Wulff Jones in 2017, and they had one child together.

Alex Jones: Career Details

Jones got his start in the business with a TV show in Austin. In 1996, he changed to radio and started a show called “The Final Edition.” Ron Paul was running for Congress, and he was a guest on his show more than once.

Jones talked about how he thought the US was behind the bombing in Oklahoma City in 1995. Jones’s first movie, “America Destroyed by Design,” came out in 1998.

The readers of The Austin Chronicle voted Jones and Shannon Burke as “Best Austin Talk Show Host” for 1999.

Jones started broadcasting from his home the following year. In 2000, Jones was one of seven Republicans running for Texas state representative. After a year, his show was broadcast on about 100 stations.

Jones started saying that the 9/11 attacks were planned by the Bush administration after they happened. It made some of the stations that used to air Jones’s show stop doing so.

Jones spoke to international media at the annual Bilderberg meeting in England in June 2013. He spoke for a long time to about 2,000 protesters in the Grove Hotel’s yard.

Since then, Jones has held protests all over the US to show what he thinks and feels about different things going on in the country.

People have said that his website, InfoWars, is a fake news site and that it spreads conspiracy theories. When Facebook banned InfoWars for breaking its rules, Jones used another site called NewsWars to get around the ban.

Alex Jones: Other Ventures

Alex made his first documentary film, “America: Destroyed by Design,” in 1998. Since then, he has made about 20 more documentaries. He has also written two books: “9-11: Descent into Tyranny” which came out in 2002, and “The Answer to 1984 Is 1776” which came out in 2008.

Jones is the owner and director of the website InfoWars, which gets about 10 million visits per month. People have said that the website is fake news and that it spreads harmful conspiracy theories. At its peak, the site made more than $20 million per year, according to reports.

In 2017, the German magazine Der Spiegel found out that two-thirds of Jones’ income comes from sales of his own products, which he promotes on his show and through the InfoWars website. Some of the things they sell are vitamins, toothpaste, bulletproof vests, and other things.

John Oliver said on a 2017 episode of “Last Week Tonight” that Jones spends almost a quarter of his on-air time promoting products sold on his website. Many of these products claim to solve medical and economic problems that Jones says are caused by the conspiracy theories he talks about on his show.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, he kept doing strange things, and on March 12, 2020, the Attorney General of New York told him to stop because he said that the products he sold could cure COVID-19 without any proof. In April 2020, the FDA got involved, and Jones was told that if he kept selling the products, the government could seize and fine him.

Alex Jones: Defamation Damages

On August 3, 2022, a jury in Austin, Texas, said that Alex had to pay the Sandy Hook family $4.1 million in damages because they had won a previous defamation trial.

On August 5, 2022, the same Austin jury told Alex to pay another $45 million in punitive damages, bringing the total amount he had to pay to just under $50 million.

Alex Jones: Controversies

Jones is known for his very controversial ideas about things like the Oklahoma City bombing, the moon landing, 9/11, Sandy Hook, Obama, Hillary Clinton, white genocide, and not getting vaccinated. Jones thinks that many terrorist attacks were “inside jobs,” which means that the US government was involved.

He thinks that climate change is a lie made up by the World Bank so that a carbon tax can be used to control the world economy. In February 2018, former employees accused Jones of sexual harassment, racism against black people, and bias against Jews.
