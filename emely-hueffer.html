Emely Hueffer (born on June 5, 1996, age 26 years) is a German makeup artist who has been gaining since she starred on Netflix’s “Too Hot To Handle: Germany.” The German version of the dating reality show aired on February 28, 2023, on the OTT giant and quickly became a hit.

There were 10 singles in the show with the ultimate goal of finding love but also trying not to engage in s*x or kiss because there was a penalty of €6,000, which was meant to be deducted from the €200,000 prize money.

It is not at all likely that you will meet your love on a dating show, but good things happened for Emely; she found her soulmate Kevin Njie. The couple even gave an interview to Glamour and accepted that they are madly in love with each other.

From Where is Emely Hueffer?

On June 5, 1996, Emely Hueffer (age 26) was born to her mother, Annette Hueffer, in Berlin, Germany. At first, we did not find much about Hueffer’s family, but later we learned that she has a sister, Annabelle, who makes music.

According to sources, the young lady spent her entire childhood in Berlin, and following high school, she developed the desire to become a makeup artist. With her mother’s permission she joined a makeup academy where she mastered the art and became a professional makeup artist.

What Does Emely Hueffer Do For a Living?

Sources claim that Emely started working as a makeup artist in Berlin, but later she shifted to Ibiza, Spain, in search of better opportunities. In 2022, Hueffer received a chance to be on the German edition of the famous Netflix dating-themed game show “Too Hot To Handle: German,” and she accepted it.

One thing which makes Emely stand out from the crowd is her unapologetic nature; before entering the dating show, she was not at all nervous infact, she was excited and curious to know if she would find her soul mate. There were men in the show, including Akka, Dennis, Fabio, and Oliver, but it was Kevin who won the heart of Berlin beauty Emely.

Following the show, during an interview, the blonde beauty described her experience on “Too Hot To Handle,” saying, “It’s about getting rid of the usual superficiality, starting human relationships differently and getting to know each other better. This is, I think, the hardest subject for most people these days. And once you open up, interesting things can happen.”
